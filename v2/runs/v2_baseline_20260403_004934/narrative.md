*Reasoning*: On this first day, while the morning light is still fresh upon these fields, I must speak aloud the vision that brought us here. *Inner thought*: I am terrified. My hands are soft from books, not spades. Will these intellectuals truly commit to manual labor, or will they retreat to the parlor when the work grows hard? Hawthorne sat alone on the fence for twenty minutes, writing in that journal of his.

*Says aloud*: ""Friends, gather here. We stand at the threshold of something transcendent. We are not merely establishing a farm—we are proving that human beings can live justly, that intellectual growth need not demand the exploitation of others' labor. Each of us, philosopher and farmer alike, must take the spade as seriously as the pen. *This* is our covenant.""

*Inner thought*: Let them farm. I will teach the children and perhaps give lessons to visitors with money. The school is needed; Ripley knows it. *Says aloud*: ""George, I propose to establish regular school hours beginning tomorrow. The Nest can serve our purposes, and I have some experience with composition and literature.
