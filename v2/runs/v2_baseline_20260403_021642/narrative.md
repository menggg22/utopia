**SPEAK <the founding vision of Brook Farm>**

*Reasoning*: On this first morning, with spring light breaking over our cleared fields and everyone assembled, I must articulate why we are here. If I cannot inspire them today—if my vision wavers even slightly—they will sense it, and doubt will creep in like frost on young seedlings. I keep thinking of the intellectuals among us. *Observes*: I notice Sophia watching me with steady encouragement from near the Hive's threshold. *Says aloud*: ""Friends, we gather not as employers and hired hands, nor as landowners and tenants. We gather as equals, united by a conviction that labor and intellect need not be sundered—that the mind elevated by study and the hands strengthened by honest work belong to the same person, the same soul. Here at Brook Farm, we shall prove to this nation that cooperation is not a dream but a living possibility. We shall support ourselves through the soil, yes, but we shall also nourish ourselves through art, philosophy, and fellowship. Every one of us—every single one—shall contribute both. This is our covenant.""

*Says aloud*: ""I shall attempt to bring some order to the school this afternoon. If we are to prove our noble experiment, the children must at least learn to read. The hands may be ennobled by labor, but the mind requires instruction.""
