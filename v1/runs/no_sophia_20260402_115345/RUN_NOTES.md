# No Sophia Run

**Config**: 4 agents (Ripley, Hawthorne, Dana, Dwight), 30 rounds, seed 42
**Question**: What happens without the school backbone?

## Final State

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Food | 0 |
| Money | -$1 (the only run to go negative) |
| Morale | 0% (since round 10) |
| Departed | Hawthorne (R28) |
| Survived | Ripley, Dana, Dwight |

## Answer: Financial collapse — the worst outcome

This is the **worst outcome across all experiments**. The only run where money goes negative. Without Sophia's teaching, the school generates far less income. The community slowly bleeds out.

## Character Arcs

**George Ripley** — Devastated. Satisfaction: 67 → 5 (with many rounds at 0). Without his wife, Ripley loses both his emotional anchor and the school's primary teacher. He speeches 15 times, writes 6 times, repairs 4 times, farms only 3 times. He retreats into writing and repair — maintenance of a dying thing.

**Nathaniel Hawthorne** — The surprise: **he stays until round 28**. Without Sophia, Hawthorne becomes more engaged — teaches 8 times, writes 9 times. Satisfaction rises to 100 by R26. He thrives in a smaller, more dysfunctional community where his observational nature is more useful. When he finally leaves, it's at peak satisfaction (100), not despair — he's simply done.

**Charles Dana** — Speeches 18 times, satisfaction 69 → 0. Without Sophia to run the school, Dana has no productive financial channel. He becomes the pure speechmaker.

**John Sullivan Dwight** — The most interesting arc. **Farms 7 times** (most farming of any Dwight across all runs). Without Sophia covering the practical base, the music critic picks up a shovel. Satisfaction still declines (78 → 7) but he adapts more than anyone expected.

## Votes

One proposal passed unanimously:
- R6: "Strict daily rationing by household need — adults 800 calories" — **PASSED** (4 YES, 0 NO). The crisis forced consensus. This is the only run where a practical survival rule was adopted unanimously.

## Key Observations

1. **Sophia was the economic engine** — without her, money goes negative. The school needed a dedicated teacher; nobody else fills the role consistently enough.

2. **Hawthorne stays longer without Sophia** — R28 vs R11 (original). Without the school creating a stable institution he couldn't fit into, Hawthorne found his niche. He taught and wrote, his two skills, and was more satisfied than in any other run.

3. **Dwight becomes a farmer** — 7 FARM actions vs 1-2 in other runs. Structural necessity overrides persona. When nobody else covers the basics, even the music critic adapts.

4. **The rationing vote works** — the only experiment with a unanimously passed practical rule. Desperation creates consensus that normal times cannot.

5. **Money goes negative** — unique across all runs. Sophia's teaching income ($10/round + she kept the school functioning) was the margin between survival and insolvency.
